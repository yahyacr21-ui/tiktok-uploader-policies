Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 17, 2026

1. Introduction

Makeshortseasy ("we", "our", or "us") is a desktop application that helps users authenticate with TikTok and upload content through TikTok's official APIs. This Privacy Policy explains what information we access, how it is used, how it is stored, and what choices users have regarding their data.

2. Information We Access

Makeshortseasy only accesses information that is explicitly authorized by the user through TikTok Login Kit and related TikTok APIs.

  • Basic profile information, such as account identity information made available by TikTok after authorization.
  • Authorization data, including OAuth authorization codes, access tokens, and refresh tokens required to maintain the connection between the user and the application.
  • User-selected media and captions that the user chooses to upload from their own device to TikTok.
  • Operational logs and error messages generated locally on the user's device for troubleshooting and debugging.

3. How We Use Information

We use authorized data only for the following purposes:

  • To authenticate the user with TikTok.
  • To maintain a valid authorized session using TikTok access and refresh tokens.
  • To upload user-selected content to the user's TikTok account through TikTok's official APIs.
  • To display upload status, success messages, and related account connection information inside the application.
  • To diagnose local technical issues and improve application stability.

4. Local Storage and Token Handling

Makeshortseasy is designed as a desktop application. Authentication tokens are stored locally on the user's device and are not transmitted to our own servers. TikTok API requests are sent directly from the user's device to TikTok as part of the authorized workflow.

Users are responsible for securing access to their own device and local files. We recommend that users keep their operating system and local environment secure.

5. Media Uploads

Makeshortseasy does not automatically collect or scan a user's files. Media is only processed or uploaded when the user explicitly selects a file and initiates the upload action within the application.

Uploaded media and captions are transmitted to TikTok only when the user chooses to publish or export content.

6. Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, or share personal information with third parties for advertising purposes.

Information may be transmitted to TikTok when necessary to complete the authorized login and content posting flow. Makeshortseasy does not operate a remote backend that stores user tokens or uploaded content on our own servers.

7. Data Retention

Makeshortseasy does not maintain centralized cloud storage of user data. Any locally stored authentication data remains on the user's device until it expires, is refreshed, or is manually removed by the user.

Local logs or temporary files may remain on the device for troubleshooting purposes unless deleted by the user or overwritten during normal application use.

8. Revoking Access and Deleting Data

Users may revoke Makeshortseasy's access to their TikTok account through their TikTok account settings, where connected apps can be managed.

Users may also delete locally stored tokens, logs, or temporary data directly from their own device at any time. After revocation or deletion, the application will no longer be able to act on behalf of the user unless the user completes authorization again.

9. Security

We take reasonable measures to reduce risk in the local authorization flow, including the use of OAuth state validation and token exchange mechanisms supported by TikTok. However, no method of local storage or electronic transmission is completely risk-free, and users are responsible for maintaining the security of their own device.

10. Children's Privacy

Makeshortseasy is not intended for children or for users who are not legally permitted to use TikTok's services and APIs under applicable law or TikTok's policies.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect application changes, legal updates, or TikTok API integration requirements. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page indicates the most recent revision.
